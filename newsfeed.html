42nd Street Moon

9/28/2019


42nd Street Moon is producing HOT MIKADO, which is based on THE MIKADO. Asian American theatre makers in the Bay Area did a great deal of work in protest of Lamplighters Music Theatre's production of THE MIKADO in 2016, which led to widespread change surrounding that show. This year a few actors shared their feelings of discomfort surrounding HOT MIKADO. We don't know the script of HOT MIKADO that well and aren't sure if it's as racist as its predecessor, but it seems questionable.

If it is as racist as THE MIKADO, it shouldn't be produced.


Theatre, Individual, Microaggression







Silhouette Productions "Music Man" in Cleveland, Ohio

9/28/2019


I played Alma Hicks in the musical "Music Man" for Silhouette Productions. I was one of three black people in a cast of about 50, and the only that was a "Pick-a-little" girl. The director asked to run something with the Pick-a-littles and an older man in the cast said "you mean pickaninis?" And walked away laughing, pretending not to hear me ask him not to say that. I emailed the director, music director, and producer about the incident. The director, a younger white man, was very apologetic, admitted he heard it in rehearsal before but didn't know what it meant, and wanted to handle it as a group in the next rehearsal (which was first tech). He then called back saying the producer didn't want it handled as a group to not "stir up animosity/rumors/etc" and to have the man just personal apologize to me. It took him three days to approach me, saying he didn't know what it meant; producer never said a word to me about it, barely talked to me at all unless it was about "business".

It should be public knowledge that they don't handle issues such as this well. I shared it with the other cast members of color.

I believe I am fine. I refuse to work there or see shows there, even though I did meet some friendly people during the production.

Tryon Little Theatre, Tryon, NC

9/19/2019


The Book Club Play by Karen Zacarias specifically calls for one of the characters to be played by an African-American and from the looks of the press shots, they had an all-white cast. I called the theatre to ask about it and I got the box office who said they did not know the details of the casting and if permission was granted by the playwright. They said they would pass my contact info on to the director who would get back to me but that never happened. I called twice more and got the voicemail so I left a message but never heard back from anyone.

I'm okay. This incident took place at a theatre I have no association with. I served as the dramaturg to a production of The Book Club Play which is why I am aware of the casting specifics.

The wishes of the playwright should always be respected.
